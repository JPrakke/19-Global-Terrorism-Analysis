Additional Data Vizualizations

When building our data analysis, we had many more questions we wanted to address, but they did not fit clearly with the message we were interpreting through our dashboard. This page contains a few additional vizualizations and information regarding global terrorism from 2000 to 2016.

Attack By Region

The Middle East and South Asia are historically where the most terrorist activity occurs. Our initial analysis reported that these were already dense with terrorist activity, but the spike of 2012 mentioned in our original analysis mentioned the Taliban reaching out to other groups in Asia to spread their wave of terror; and this graph shows that increase in both the Middle East states, and southern Asian states. It's also important to note the spike in activity in eastern Europe, largely can be attribute to the immense amount of activity mentioned in our initial analysis from Greece.

Top Ten Countries by Casualty Likeliness (Predicted)

A break down of the top ten countries most likely to have a terrorist attack. These values were predicted using the machine learning model Random Forest Classifier. We recieved a very high accuracy score on this model. Most of it comes from the Middle East and Asia as expected, however what is staggering is that out of 164 countries listed in our database, these ten countries alone have a 67. 4% chance of having a casualty of a terrorist attack in them. 6.1% of countries in our database have a 67.4% chance of having the next casualty of a terrorist attack.

Top Seven Regions by Casualty Likeliness (Predicted)

Using machine learning, we were able to predict the likeliness of being the next country to have a casualty to a terrorist attack. The model used was a Random Forest Classifier, and gave us a very high score on accuracy. One interesting thing to notice is while a European country does not appear in our pie chart of the top ten countries most likely to have a terrorist casualty next, our model predicts that there is a 10.5% chance of it having the next casualty of a terrorist attack. With rising tensions over immigration control and how refugees are handled, we have seen increasing amounts of large scale acts of terrorism on the European public such as the November 2015 Paris attacks. Our model predicted America as the #31 spot, estimating that there was a 5.1% chance of having the next casualty of a terrorist attack.
